Stop Reminders Using Google Maps on an Android Phone

If you set up public transit navigation in Google Maps, the program will alert you when it is time to get off the bus. But sometimes you're already on the bus by the time you realize you want a reminder. Google Maps can do this too, but it's not quite as obvious; here's how to make it work on Google Maps using an Android phone.

Step 1
In Google Maps, find the stop at which you want to exit the bus, and tap it.
 
Step 2
Tap on the name bubble for that stop.
 
Step 3
Tap on the icon for "Directions".
 
Step 4
Usually "My Location" will already be selected, but go ahead and make this selection if necessary.
Make sure the "public transit" icon is selected.
Tap on "Get Directions".
 
Step 5
The program will assume you're not already on the bus, and will therefore show you trips in the future – but just scroll to the bottom and tap "Show earlier trips".
 
Step 6
You'll see a list of trips that have already begun; tap the one corresponding to the bus that you're on. (In my example, it was 5:00pm and I was on the 36, so I clicked the first item in the list: Bus 36 from 4:50pm - 5:10pm.)
 
Step 7
This will bring up the directions. Tap the "Navigation" arrow to let Google Maps know you want to use navigation. (Pro Tip: inside Navigation, you can switch between the map view and the text view with an icon in the bottom; in text view, you can bring up Settings > Notification Settings, which will allow you to turn off voice alerts, which may be embarrassing on a crowded bus. I prefer to use only vibration alerts, but your choices are vibration, voice, and ringtone.)
 
Step 8
Google Maps will warn you that you're getting information for a run that has already started. Since this is exactly what you want, click "Proceed anyway".

Then just relax, read a book, rest your eyes, or chat with a friend; your phone will alert you when your stop is the next one.
